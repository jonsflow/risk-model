Fed Chair Transition: Powell → Warsh

Kevin Warsh's Senate confirmation hearing concluded April 21, 2026 — he is expected to be seated as Fed Chair in the coming weeks. His framework is a genuine regime change: strict 2% inflation target with no averaging, the balance sheet as a co-equal policy tool for aggressive QT, and elimination of the dot plot and quarterly forward guidance. This page tracks the four indicators that define his mandate.
Powell Era (2018–2026)
Flexible Average Inflation Targeting
  • FAIT (2020–2025): deliberately allowed inflation to run above 2%
  • Quarterly SEP + dot plot as primary forward guidance tool
  • Balance sheet used as secondary stimulus lever (QE)
  • Gradual QT from ~$9T peak at ~$95B/month cap
  • Strict 2% target restored at Jackson Hole Aug 2025
  • Reactive: cut 50bps Sep 2024 despite Core PCE above 2%
Warsh Framework (Expected 2026+)
"Inflation is a choice"
  • Strict 2% target — no averaging, no overshoot tolerance
  • Eliminate dot plot and quarterly forward guidance
  • Balance sheet = co-equal tool: aggressive QT as primary lever
  • "QT for cuts": drain balance sheet, allow rate cuts simultaneously
  • Focus on underlying/trend inflation, not transitory swings
  • Return to "scarce reserves" — reduce Fed footprint in markets
"If the Fed had kept a smaller balance sheet… I think interest rates could be lower, inflation could be better and the economy could be stronger." — Kevin Warsh, Senate Banking Committee, April 21, 2026

What to Expect Under Warsh

Balance Sheet (QT)
Expect QT to accelerate beyond Powell's gradual pace. Balance sheet reduction becomes a primary policy lever, not a background process running on autopilot.
"QT for Cuts"
Rate cuts tied to balance sheet shrinkage — not just inflation data. As QE-era liquidity drains, Warsh believes short rates can fall without reigniting inflation.
No More Dot Plot
The quarterly Summary of Economic Projections (dot plot) is expected to be eliminated at or shortly after his first FOMC meeting. Markets lose a major rate-path signal.
Inflation Measurement
Focus on underlying/trend inflation rather than headline prints driven by geopolitical or one-time factors. Skeptical of "transitory" framing that defined 2021.
